Operationalizing the Four-Term Decomposition on AutoResearch:
Architecture Choice for Agentic Training Research
under Wall-Clock and Cost Budgets
Abstract
AutoResearch [2] is a concrete instance of a budgeted transductive task with a noisy scalar
verifier: an agentic system modifies a single training script and is judged by held-out validation
bits-per-byteafterafixedfive-minutetrainingrunonasingleH100. WeapplytheBeneventano–
Poggio (BP) framework [1] to five architecture-choice questions: what “better” means, when
one architecture beats another, why, by how much, and whether the answer can be predicted
in advance. Three minimal extensions to BP are required and no more: (i) splitting certified
timeintowall-clockandcostaxes,(ii)introducingalatentlosstoabsorbverifiernoise, and(iii)
folding context pressure into the per-step cost. The four-term decomposition ∆=log(κ /κ)+
0
φ+G−εholdsonbothaxeswiththesamethreeexplanatorytermsandabifurcatedcostterm.
We instantiate the framework on the stage-1 2×2 memory/parallelism ablation and specify a
measurement protocol with falsifiable predictions.
1 Introduction
The meeting that motivated this note posed five architecture-choice questions for agentic systems
on verifiable tasks:
Q1. What does it mean for architecture A to be better than architecture B?
Q2. When is A better than B?
Q3. Why is A better than B?
Q4. By how much is A better than B?
Q5. Can we predict in advance when A will be better than B?
Beneventano and Poggio (BP) [1] answer analogous questions in the setting of formal theorem
proving in Lean 4, using a budgeted transductive task formalism, a packed latent-mode family, and
a four-term decomposition
κ
0
∆ = log + φ + G − ε

that separates per-step cost, within-mode competence, information generation, and routing mis-
match into independently measurable channels. Their setting assumes a deterministic verifier (the
Lean kernel), a single fused certified time that collapses wall-clock and cost, and an implicit treat-
ment of context budget.
AutoResearch is structurally close but differs in three ways that matter for the meeting’s ques-
tions. The verifier, val bpb after five minutes of training, is scalar and noisy. Architecture choice
centers on parallel agents and shared workspace rather than model choice, which makes wall-clock
genuinely distinct from cost. And the agent’s context fills across attempts, which affects per-step
latency in a way BP’s constant κ cannot express.

3 Two certified times: wall-clock and cost
BP defines a single certified time TA(n,δ). For AutoResearch, two things force a split. First,
the five-minute training is a fixed wall-clock block per attempt, independent of the number of
concurrent agents proposing in parallel. Second, running two agents in parallel roughly halves the
wall-clock path for a given number of completed attempts but leaves the total token and GPU-hour
cost approximately unchanged (in fact slightly higher due to coordination overhead). A single TA
cannot express both facts.

6 Answers to the five meeting questions
We can now address each question directly.
6.1 Q1. What does “better” mean?
Architecture d dominates d′ at target q⋆ if it is at least as good on both axes:
Td (q⋆,δ) ≤ Td′ (q⋆,δ) and Td (q⋆,δ) ≤ Td′ (q⋆,δ),
wall wall cost cost
with strict inequality on at least one. When neither dominates, we describe the pair as a Pareto
trade-off. Equivalently, d dominates d′ if and only if ∆ (d | d′) ≥ 0 and ∆ (d | d′) ≥ 0, with
wall cost
strict sign on at least one.
This is strictly stronger than a single-axis comparison and weaker than a scalar utility; it
matches the meeting’s insistence that the two axes be kept distinct. A paper reporting only wall-
clock speedup hides cost-axis losses, and vice versa; reporting both makes Pareto disagreements
visible and informative.

7 Measurement protocol for the 2 × 2 pilot
The decomposition is useful only if the four terms can be estimated from AutoResearch runs. We
specify a protocol in the style of BP Section 9.
7.1 Mode labeling
Pool all successful edits (those that improved the latent val bpb above the pre-edit incumbent)
across development-set runs of d and d . Apply a domain-specific similarity metric: syntactic-

tree distance on train.py diffs, augmented with keyword tags (optim., lr, arch, reg, data).

in the 4–8 range, compatible with Assumption 1. Fix labels before any model-dependent quantity
is computed. Sensitivity to the partition is checked by rerunning the analysis with ±1 merged or
split clusters.

7.7 Negative-result criterion
If, after the pilot, φˆ, Gˆ, and εˆfor the 2×2 cells do not exceed their bootstrap uncertainty, and the
raw best-so-far curves in Conditions A and B are explainable by a single scalar efficiency metric

7.8 Rough budget
At Karpathy’s default pace of roughly 12 attempts/hour per agent, with a baseline of about $0.02
per attempt in agent tokens plus $0.20 per attempt in GPU (at an H100 spot rate of $2.40/hour),
a full 2×2 pilot under both conditions, run to 200 attempts per cell, costs approximately $180 in
compute and $50 in agent tokens, for a total near $230, plus the engineering cost of the logging
harness and re-evaluation protocol. This is within the screening heuristic of BP Section 9.2.
8 Discussion
What the framework provides. Threethings. First, aformal answerto “better” thatrespects
the wall-clock/cost distinction the meeting insisted on. Second, an explanatory decomposition that
traces each design variable to a measurable channel, so that “why did memory help” is not a vibe
but a number. Third, predictive crossover formulas for each of the three design contrasts on each
of the two axes, with only three structural quantities (κ¯, ε, G) needing estimation from a pilot.
What it does not provide. Theframeworkdoesnotsolvethejointdesignoptimization. Itdoes
nottellthebuilderhowtosetpromptingstyle, toolselection, ormemoryschemainadvance—those
remain engineering choices. It does not predict the result without a pilot; it structures the pilot’s
outputs so that a small study generalizes to nearby design points. And it does not cover cross-task
transfer, since AutoResearch is a singleton task.
Sensitivity to the mode partition. The six-mode partition of Assumption 1 is a stipula-
tion, not a discovery. If the empirical clustering of Section 7 produces a substantially different
partition—say, four modes or nine—the numerical values of G and ε will change, but the quali-
tative decomposition remains valid as long as the partition is stable across bootstrap resamples.
Predictions that flip sign under ±1 cluster perturbation should be marked as unstable.
What the pilot will tell us. Ifthedecompositionandcrossoverpredictionshold, AutoResearch
becomes the empirical foundation for extending BP beyond Lean: it will have been shown that
mode-conditioned reasoning works in continuous-verifier settings with noisy evaluation. If the
predictions fail—for instance, if context pressure turns out not to be the dominant mechanism, or

The shortest honest summary of this note is: the meeting’s five questions have clean answers
in BP’s language once wall-clock and cost are decoupled. The rest is instrumentation.
